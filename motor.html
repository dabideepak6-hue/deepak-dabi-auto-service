What is an EV Motor?

An electric motor converts electrical energy from the battery into mechanical rotation to drive the vehicle.

  • ✔ High Efficiency
  • ✔ Low Maintenance
  • ✔ Instant Torque
  • ✔ Regenerative Braking Support

Motor Types

BLDC Motor

Brushless DC Motor used in many electric scooters.

PMSM Motor

Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor provides smooth and efficient performance.

Hub Motor

Motor integrated directly inside the wheel.

Mid Drive Motor

Motor mounted in the chassis and connected through transmission.

Main Motor Components

Rotor

Contains permanent magnets and rotates with the shaft.

Stator

Contains copper windings that create the rotating magnetic field.

Hall Sensors

Provide rotor position information to the controller.

Bearings

Support smooth shaft rotation and reduce friction.

Motor Cable

Three phase wires (U, V, W) and Hall sensor connector.

Motor Shaft

Transfers mechanical power to the wheel.

Motor Inspection Checklist

  • ✅ Visual Inspection
  • ✅ Motor Cable Check
  • ✅ Connector Inspection
  • ✅ Bearing Noise
  • ✅ Shaft Rotation
  • ✅ Hall Sensor Test
  • ✅ Winding Resistance
  • ✅ Insulation Test

Common Motor Faults

Motor Not Running

  • Hall Sensor Fault
  • Broken Phase Wire
  • Controller Fault
  • Loose Connector

Motor Jerking

  • Hall Sensor Failure
  • Loose Wiring
  • Controller Issue

Motor Heating

  • Overload
  • Bearing Damage
  • Shorted Winding

Abnormal Noise

  • Bearing Damage
  • Rotor Contact
  • Loose Mounting

Motor Testing Procedure

  1. Switch OFF Vehicle
  2. Disconnect Battery
  3. Inspect Motor Connector
  4. Measure Phase Resistance (U-V, V-W, U-W)
  5. Check Hall Sensor Supply
  6. Rotate Wheel by Hand
  7. Perform Insulation Test
  8. Check Bearing Condition
  9. Repair or Replace Faulty Parts
  10. Reassemble and Road Test

Hall Sensor Signal

Wire Typical Voltage
Red +5V Supply
Black Ground
Yellow 0V - 5V Signal
Green 0V - 5V Signal
Blue 0V - 5V Signal

Technician Safety

  • ⚠ Disconnect Battery Before Motor Work.
  • ⚠ Never Rotate Motor with Live Exposed Wiring.
  • ⚠ Use Insulated Tools.
  • ⚠ Keep Water Away from Motor Connectors.
  • ⚠ Tighten Mounting Bolts to Manufacturer Torque.
