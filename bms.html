⚡ Battery Management System (BMS)

Learn Complete BMS Diagnosis, Testing & Repair

What is BMS?

Battery Management System (BMS) is the electronic control unit that monitors and protects the lithium battery pack. Main Functions:

  • ✔ Cell Voltage Monitoring
  • ✔ Cell Balancing
  • ✔ Temperature Monitoring
  • ✔ Over Charge Protection
  • ✔ Over Discharge Protection
  • ✔ Short Circuit Protection
  • ✔ Over Current Protection
  • ✔ CAN Communication

Main BMS Components

Battery Cells

Individual Lithium Cells connected in Series and Parallel.

MOSFET

Controls Charging and Discharging Current.

NTC Sensor

Measures Battery Temperature.

Voltage Sense IC

Measures Cell Voltage Accurately.

Current Sensor

Measures Charge and Discharge Current.

Microcontroller

Processes Data and Controls Protection Logic.

BMS Inspection Checklist

  • ✅ Battery Voltage
  • ✅ Individual Cell Voltage
  • ✅ Cell Balancing
  • ✅ MOSFET Condition
  • ✅ Temperature Sensor
  • ✅ Fuse Check
  • ✅ CAN Communication
  • ✅ Connector Condition

Common BMS Faults

Battery Not Charging

  • Faulty MOSFET
  • Fuse Open
  • BMS Lock
  • Broken Connector

Battery Not Discharging

  • Protection Active
  • Output MOSFET Damage
  • Communication Failure

Battery Heating

  • Weak Cell
  • High Current
  • MOSFET Heating

Cell Imbalance

  • Weak Cell
  • Balancing Circuit Fault
  • Damaged Cell

BMS Repair Procedure

  1. Switch OFF Vehicle
  2. Disconnect Battery Pack
  3. Wear HV Safety Gloves
  4. Measure Total Battery Voltage
  5. Measure Every Cell Voltage
  6. Check Fuse
  7. Inspect MOSFET
  8. Check NTC Sensor
  9. Check Current Sensor
  10. Check CAN Communication
  11. Repair / Replace Faulty Components
  12. Reassemble Battery
  13. Charge Battery
  14. Road Test

Typical Cell Voltage Guide

Condition Voltage
Fully Charged 4.20V
Normal 3.60V - 4.00V
Low Battery 3.20V
Critical Below 3.00V

Technician Safety

  • ⚠ Never Short Circuit Battery Terminals.
  • ⚠ Use Only Insulated Tools.
  • ⚠ Wear HV Gloves.
  • ⚠ Never Open Live Battery.
  • ⚠ Disconnect Charger Before Repair.
  • ⚠ Check Insulation Before Assembly.
